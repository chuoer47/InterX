## Important Safeguards

When using electrical appliances, basic safety precautions should always be followed.

1. Read this entire document before operating the appliance. Failure to follow these safeguards and instructions may result in injury and/or property damage.
2. Use only the Instant Pot Duo Crisp pressure cooking lid and Duo Crisp air fryer lid with the Instant Pot Duo Crisp cooker base.
3. Cook only in an authorised stainless-steel Instant Pot inner pot. Do not use the appliance without the removable inner pot installed.
4. Always use an Instant Pot stainless-steel inner pot when using the air fryer lid. Do not use a ceramic coated inner pot.
5. When pressure cooking, do not open the appliance until it has depressurised and all internal pressure has been released. If the float valve is still up or the lid is difficult to open, the appliance is still pressurised.
6. Always operate the appliance on a stable, non-combustible, level surface.
7. Use the appliance only for its intended use.
8. For household use only. Do not use outdoors. Not for commercial use.
9. Do not place the appliance on or near a hot gas or electric burner, heated oven, water, flame, or direct sunlight.
10. Do not touch hot surfaces. Use side handles for carrying or moving.
11. The cooker base and air fryer lid contain electrical components. Do not put liquid into the cooker base or air fryer lid, and do not immerse the power cord, plug, or appliance in water.
12. Do not fill over the `PC MAX - 2/3` line. When cooking foods that expand, such as rice or dried vegetables, do not fill over the `- 1/2` line.
13. Make certain the appliance is properly closed before pressure cooking.
14. Do not use the appliance for deep frying or pressure frying with oil.
15. Do not lean over or place hands or face over the steam release valve or float valve when the appliance is in operation or has residual pressure.
16. Do not cover or obstruct the steam release valve or float valve.
17. Turn the appliance off if steam escapes from the steam release valve, float valve, or sides of the lid in an abnormal way. Let the appliance depressurise before opening.
18. To disconnect, press `Cancel`, then remove the plug from the power source.
19. Unplug when not in use and before cleaning.
20. Regularly inspect the appliance and power cord. Do not operate if the cord or plug is damaged, or if the appliance malfunctions, is dropped, or is damaged.
21. Keep the appliance and cord away from children. Never drape the cord over table or counter edges. Do not use below-counter outlets or extension cords.
22. Keep vents on the cooker base and air fryer lid unblocked. Leave at least 13 cm of space around the air fryer lid when in use.
23. Do not use unauthorised accessories or attachments.
24. Before inserting the inner pot, wipe the outside of the inner pot and the heating element with a clean, dry cloth.
25. Do not attempt to repair, replace, modify, or tamper with any appliance component or safety mechanism.
26. Do not use the appliance with power converters or adapters.
27. Check the steam release valve, steam release pipe, anti-block shield, float valve, heating element, and air fryer lid element cover before and after use.
28. Do not overfill the air fryer basket.
29. Do not connect the appliance to an external timer switch or separate remote-control system.
30. Use heat-resistant hand protection when removing accessories or handling the hot inner pot.
31. While cooking, the internal temperature reaches several hundred degrees. Do not place unprotected hands inside the appliance until it has cooled.
32. Keep hands and face away from air vents when the air fryer lid is operating.
33. If the unit emits black smoke when using the air fryer lid, press `Cancel`, unplug the appliance, wait for smoke to stop, then clean thoroughly.
34. Do not leave the appliance unattended while in use.
35. Do not store materials in the cooker base or inner pot when not in use.
36. Do not place combustible materials such as paper, cardboard, plastic, Styrofoam, or wood in the cooker base or inner pot.
37. Do not use included accessories in a microwave, toaster oven, convection or conventional oven, ceramic cooktop, electric coil, gas range, or outdoor grill.
38. Use caution when lifting a full inner pot.
39. Use caution when removing the lid. Heat and steam escape as soon as the lid is removed.

## Initial Setup

Remove packaging material and accessories from in and around the cooker. Check under the inner pot.

Wash the inner pot in a dishwasher or with hot water and dish soap. Rinse with warm, clear water and dry the outside thoroughly with a soft cloth.

Wipe the heating element with a soft, dry cloth.

Do not remove safety stickers from the lid or the rating label from the back of the cooker base.

Place the cooker base on a stable, level surface away from combustible material and external heat sources.

## Overview of Pressure Control Features

### Pressure Cooking Lid

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_11.jpg)

To remove the lid:

1. Grip the lid handle and turn counterclockwise to align the symbol on the lid with the symbol on the rim of the cooker base.
2. Lift the lid up and off the cooker base toward your body.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_12.jpg)

To close the lid:

1. Align the symbol on the lid with the symbol on the cooker base and lower the lid onto the track.
2. Turn the lid clockwise until the symbol on the lid aligns with the symbol on the cooker base.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_13.jpg)

### Quick Release Button

The quick release button controls the steam release valve and whether pressure is released.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_14.jpg)

The lid says `Turn to Seal`, but the motion is a flick. Do not turn the quick release button more than 1/4 turn, or 45 degrees. Let it spring back to its original position.

### Steam Release Valve

The steam release valve sits loosely on the steam release pipe. When the cooker releases pressure, steam ejects from the top of the steam release valve.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_15.jpg)

Do not cover or block the steam release valve.

### Sealing Ring

When the pressure cooking lid is closed, the sealing ring creates an airtight seal between the lid and the inner pot. The sealing ring must be installed before using the cooker. Install only one sealing ring at a time.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_16.jpg)

Only use authorised Instant Pot sealing rings. Do not use a stretched or damaged sealing ring. Replace the sealing ring every 12-18 months, or sooner if it is stretched, deformed, or damaged.

### Float Valve

The float valve visually indicates whether the cooker is pressurised.

Pressurised: the float valve has popped up above the lid surface.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_17.jpg)

Depressurised: the float valve has descended and is flush with the lid.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_18.jpg)

Do not attempt to remove the lid while the float valve is up.

### Anti-Block Shield

The anti-block shield prevents food particles from coming up through the steam release pipe and assists pressure regulation.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_19.jpg)

## Depressurising the Cooker

You must release pressure after pressure cooking and before opening the lid. Follow the recipe instructions to choose a venting method.

### Natural Release (NR)

Natural Release stops cooking gradually. As the temperature in the cooker drops, the cooker depressurises naturally over time.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_9.jpg)

Use Natural Release after cooking high-starch foods such as soups, stews, chilis, pasta, oatmeal, and congee, or foods that expand, such as beans and grains.

### Quick Release (QR)

Quick Release stops cooking quickly and helps prevent overcooking. Use it for quick-cooking vegetables and delicate seafood.

Press the quick release button down until it clicks and locks into the `Vent` position.

![Illustration](插图/multi-use_pressure_cooker_and_air_fryer_01.jpg)

When pressed down, steam ejects through the top of the steam release valve. When pressure has fully released, the float valve drops into the lid.

![Illustration](插图/multi-use_pressure_cooker_and_air_fryer_02.jpg)

Do not place hands, face, or exposed skin over the steam release valve.

## Initial Test Run

1. Remove the inner pot from the cooker base and add 750 mL of water.
2. Place the inner pot back into the cooker base.
3. Connect the power cord to a 220-240 V power source. The display shows `OFF`.
4. Place and close the pressure cooking lid.
5. Select `Pressure Cook`.
6. Use the `- / +` time buttons to adjust the cooking time to 5 minutes.
7. Press `Keep Warm` to turn automatic Keep Warm off.
8. Press `Start`. The display shows `On`.
9. When the programme finishes, the display shows `End`.
10. Follow Quick Release instructions.
11. Wait for the float valve to drop, then carefully open and remove the lid.
12. Using hand protection, remove the inner pot, discard the water, and dry the inner pot.

## Smart Programmes: Non Air Frying

### Pressure Cook and Steam

Pressure Cook and Steam use pressurised steam to cook food evenly.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_20.jpg)

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_21.jpg)

Use water-based liquids such as broth, stock, soup, or juice. If using condensed or cream-based soup, add water as directed.

Do not fill the inner pot above the `PC MAX - 2/3` line. For foods that expand, do not fill above the `- 1/2` line.

### Slow Cook

Slow Cook is comparable to a traditional slow cooker and can be used with slow cooker recipes. The float valve should not rise during Slow Cook.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_22.jpg)

### Sauté

Sauté can be used in place of a frying pan, griddle, or flat-top grill. When pressure cooking after Sauté, deglaze the inner pot.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_23.jpg)

To deglaze, remove food items, add a thin liquid such as water, broth, or wine to the hot surface, and loosen stuck food with a wooden or silicone scraper.

Do not use the lid and do not leave the cooker unattended while using Sauté.

### Sous Vide

Sous Vide cooks food underwater in an airtight, food-safe bag for a long period of time.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_24.jpg)

## Smart Programmes: Air Frying

Air Fry, Roast, Bake, Grill, and Dehydrate use the air fryer lid.

Always ensure the stainless-steel inner pot is present in the cooker base when using the air fryer lid. Do not use a non-stick ceramic coated inner pot.

![Illustration](插图/multi-use_pressure_cooker_and_air_fryer_03.jpg)

> Air-frying overview image: the display/control panel example for air-frying programmes.

![Illustration](插图/multi-use_pressure_cooker_and_air_fryer_04.jpg)

> Air-frying overview image: the air fryer lid seated on the cooker base.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_25.jpg)

> Air-frying reference table: suggested accessories, default times, temperature ranges, and notes for air-frying Smart Programmes.

1. Insert food into the multi-level air fryer basket, oven-safe cookware, or directly into the inner pot.
2. Insert the stainless-steel inner pot into the cooker base.
3. Place the air fryer lid on the cooker base by lining up the lid fins and inserting them into the base handles.
4. Press the lid down to make sure the power and sensor connectors are in full contact.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_26.jpg)

5. Select `Air Fry`, `Grill`, `Bake`, `Roast`, or `Dehydrate`.
6. Use `+ / -` to adjust time and temperature.
7. Press `Start`. The display shows `On` while the air fryer lid preheats.
8. During Air Fry and Roast, the cooker beeps partway through cooking and shows `turn Food`.
9. If removing the lid, carefully lift it off the cooker base and place it on the protective pad. Lifting the lid pauses cooking.
10. Turn, flip, or rotate the food, then place the lid back on the cooker base.
11. When the programme completes, the cooker beeps and the display shows `End`.

## Care, Cleaning and Storage

Clean the cooker and parts after each use.

Always unplug the cooker and let it cool to room temperature before cleaning. Do not use metal scouring pads, abrasive powders, or harsh chemical detergents.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_29.jpg)

Some discoloration may occur after machine washing, but this does not affect safety or performance.

The cooker base contains electrical components. Keep it dry.

- Do not immerse the cooker base in water or other liquid.
- Do not put the cooker base in a dishwasher.
- Do not rinse the heating element.
- Do not submerge or rinse the power cord or plug.

### Silicone Sealing Ring

To remove the sealing ring, grip the edge of the silicone and pull it out from behind the stainless-steel sealing ring rack.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_30.jpg)

To install the sealing ring, place it over the sealing ring rack and press it into place.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_31.jpg)

### Steam Release Valve

To remove the steam release valve, pull it up and off the steam release pipe.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_32.jpg)

To install it, place it on the steam release pipe and press down firmly. It sits loosely when installed properly.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_33.jpg)

### Anti-Block Shield

To remove the anti-block shield, grip the lid like a steering wheel and press firmly against the side of the shield until it pops off.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_34.jpg)

To install it, place it over the prongs and press down until it snaps into position.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_35.jpg)

Do not operate the pressure cooker without the anti-block shield installed.

### Float Valve and Silicone Cap

To remove the float valve, place one finger on the flat top of the float valve, turn the lid over, detach the silicone cap, and remove the float valve from the top of the lid.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_36.jpg)

To install it, drop the narrow end into the float valve hole on top of the lid, turn the lid over, and firmly attach the silicone cap to the bottom of the float valve.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_37.jpg)

Do not operate the pressure cooker without the float valve and silicone cap properly installed.

### Condensation Collector

The condensation collector sits at the back of the cooker base and catches overflow from the condensation rim. Install it before cooking, then empty and rinse it after each use.

To remove it, pull it away from the cooker base. To install it, align the grooves over the tabs on the back of the cooker base and slide it into place.

![Illustration](插图/Manual30_38.jpg)
