## Using Your Roomba Robot Vacuum

### Positioning the Home Base Charging Station

Place the Home Base in a location with good Wi-Fi coverage.

Keep the following clearances around the Home Base:

- At least 1.5 ft (0.5 m) on both sides.
- At least 4 ft (1.2 m) in front.
- At least 4 ft (1.2 m) away from stairs.

**WARNING:** To prevent the robot from falling down stairs, place the Home Base at least 4 ft (1.2 m) away from stairs.

### Download the iRobot HOME App and Connect to Wi-Fi

Use the iRobot HOME App to:

- Follow step-by-step setup instructions.
- Set an automatic cleaning schedule and customize cleaning preferences.
- Access tips, tricks, and answers to commonly asked questions.

### Charging

The light ring indicates battery status while charging:

- Solid white: fully charged.
- Pulsing white: charging.
- Pulsing red: charging / low.

Place the robot on the Home Base to activate the battery.

**Note:** The robot comes with a partial charge, but iRobot recommends charging it for 3 hours before starting the first cleaning job.

If the robot needs to recharge mid-clean, it returns to the Home Base. Once sufficiently charged, it resumes the cleaning job where it left off.

The robot uses a small amount of power whenever it is on the Home Base. For long-term storage, turn off the robot by removing it from the Home Base and holding the Home button for 10 seconds. Store the robot in a cool, dry place.

## Cleaning

Remove excess clutter from floors before cleaning. Use the robot frequently to maintain well-conditioned floors.

Press `CLEAN` to control cleaning:

- Tap to start, pause, or resume.
- Hold for 3 seconds to end a job.

The robot automatically explores and cleans the home. It returns to the Home Base at the end of a cleaning job and whenever it needs to recharge.

To send the robot back to the Home Base, tap the Home button. A blue light ring pattern indicates that the robot is seeking the Home Base.

When the robot detects an especially dirty area, it enters Dirt Detect mode and moves over the area multiple times until it senses the area has been sufficiently cleaned. The light ring indicator flashes blue.

To spot clean a designated area, place the robot on the debris and tap Spot Clean. The robot spirals outward about 3 ft (1 m), then spirals inward to where it started.

When the robot senses that its bin needs to be emptied, the light ring indicator illuminates in a red sweeping motion toward the rear.

## Troubleshooting

The robot indicates problems by playing an audio alert and turning the light ring indicator red.

Press `CLEAN` for details. Additional support and instructional videos are available in the iRobot HOME App.

If unsure whether the robot is finished or paused, consult the app for current status.

## Battery Safety & Shipping

For best results, use only the iRobot lithium ion battery that comes with the robot.

**WARNING:** Lithium ion batteries and products containing lithium ion batteries are subject to transportation regulations. For service, travel, or shipping, follow the shipping instructions.

- The battery must be turned off before shipping.
- Turn off the battery by removing the robot from the Home Base and holding the Home button for 10 seconds. All indicators turn off.
- Package the robot securely for shipping.
- Contact Customer Care or visit `global.irobot.com` if further assistance is needed.

## Accessories

### Dual Mode Virtual Wall Barrier

For best results, place the Dual Mode Virtual Wall Barrier at least 8 ft (2.5 m) from the Home Base.

Use the Dual Mode Virtual Wall Barrier to keep the robot in the places you want cleaned and out of places you do not want it to enter.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_4.jpg)

### Virtual Wall Mode

When the switch is in the Virtual Wall position, the device creates a linear barrier to block openings up to 10 ft (3 m).

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_6.jpg)

**Note:** The barrier gets wider as it gets farther from the device.

### Halo Mode

Toggle the switch to the Halo position to create a circular barrier. This prevents the robot from approaching zones such as a pet bowl, vase, or under a desk. The Halo barrier reaches approximately 12 in (30 cm) from the center of the device.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_5.jpg)

**Note:** Batteries last about 8-10 months under normal use. If you are not planning to use the Virtual Wall Barrier for an extended period, turn the switch to the middle off position.

## Care and Maintenance

To keep the robot running at peak performance, perform the following care procedures.

### Care Frequency

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_7.jpg)

| Part | Care Frequency | Replacement Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Bin | After each use | - |
| Filter | Once per week; twice per week in homes with pets | Every 2 months |
| Full Bin Sensors | Once every 2 weeks | - |
| Front Caster Wheel, Side Brush, Cliff Sensors, Charging Contacts, iAdapt Localization Camera, Floor Tracking Sensor | Once per month | - |
| Extractors | Once every 4 months; once every 3 months in homes with pets | Every 6-12 months |

### Emptying the Bin

1. Press the bin release button to remove the bin.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_8.jpg)

2. Open the bin door to empty the bin.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_9.jpg)

3. Place the bin back in the robot.

### Cleaning the Filter

1. Remove the bin. Remove the filter by grasping both ends and pulling out.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_10.jpg)

2. Remove debris by tapping the filter against a rubbish bin.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_11.jpg)

3. Reinsert the filter with ridged grips facing out. Place the bin back in the robot.

**Important:** The robot will not run if the filter is not installed correctly. Replace the filter every two months.

### Cleaning the Full Bin Sensors

1. Remove and empty the bin.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_12.jpg)

2. Wipe the inner sensors with a clean, dry cloth.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_13.jpg)

3. Wipe the bin door with a clean, dry cloth.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_14.jpg)

### Washing the Bin

**Important:** Do not wash the filter. Remove the filter before washing the bin.

1. Release the bin, remove the filter, and open the bin door.
2. Rinse the bin using warm water.
3. Make sure the bin is completely dry. Reinsert the filter and place the bin back in the robot.

**Note:** The bin is not dishwasher safe.

### Cleaning the Edge-Sweeping Brush

1. Use a small screwdriver to loosen the screw holding the Edge-Sweeping Brush in place.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_16.jpg)

2. Pull to remove the Edge-Sweeping Brush. Remove hair or debris, then reinstall the brush.

### Cleaning the Front Caster Wheel

1. Pull firmly on the front wheel module to remove it from the robot.
2. Pull firmly on the wheel to remove it from its housing.
3. Remove debris from inside the wheel cavity.
4. Reinstall all parts when finished. Make sure the wheel clicks back into place.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_15.jpg)

**Important:** A front wheel clogged with hair and debris could damage the floor. If the wheel does not spin freely after cleaning, contact Customer Care.

### Cleaning the Multi-Surface Brushes

1. Pinch the brush frame release tab, lift the tab, and remove any obstructions.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_19.jpg)

2. Remove the brushes from the robot. Remove the brush caps from the ends of the brushes. Remove hair or debris that has collected beneath the caps. Reinstall the brush caps.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_20.jpg)

3. Remove hair and debris from the square and hexagonal pegs on the opposite side of the brushes.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_21.jpg)

4. Remove the bin from the robot and clear debris from the vacuum path.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_22.jpg)

5. Reinstall the brushes in the robot. Match the shape of the brush pegs with the shape of the brush icons in the cleaning head module.

### Cleaning the Sensors and Charging Contacts

Wipe sensors and charging contacts with a clean, dry cloth. Gently use a pad of melamine foam to remove built-up debris on the robot and Home Base charging contacts.

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_17.jpg)

![Illustration](插图/Manual32_18.jpg)

**Important:** Do not spray cleaning solution or water onto the sensors or sensor openings.
