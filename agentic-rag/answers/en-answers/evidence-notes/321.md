# Evidence Notes: 321

Question: Can you share me some safety tips using the grill?

Manual used: `agentic-rag/en-manual/Outdoor Grill.md`

Status: answered

Evidence:

- `Outdoor Grill.md:21-29` gives the gas-odor response: shut off gas, extinguish open flame, open lid, and call the gas supplier or fire department if odor continues.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:65-75` covers installation precautions, including LP propane use, keeping cords and fuel hoses away from hot surfaces, and not using or modifying the grill for RVs or boats.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:198-212` and `Outdoor Grill.md:252-272` cover leak testing: repeat after LP tank exchange/refill, do it outdoors with soap and water, do not smoke or use flame, and do not use the grill if leaks are found or cannot be stopped.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:276-290` lists safe-use rules: keep children away, keep the area clear, do not block bottom/back holes, check burner flames, use only in a well-ventilated outdoor space, and maintain the stated clearances.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:294-308` gives the named Safety Tips section: check coupling nut, turn off knobs and LP tank valve when not in use, do not move a hot/operating grill, use long-handled utensils and oven mitts, observe shelf and pot-size limits, keep the grease tray installed and empty it after cooling, stop and inspect/leak-test if grease drips onto gas parts, and wait 5 minutes after failed ignition.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:310-318` and `Outdoor Grill.md:320-344` cover apartment/balcony and lighting cautions: use ground-floor outdoor clearance if allowed, never light with lid closed, open lid while lighting, do not lean over the grill, and wait 5 minutes if ignition fails.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:354-358` says not to use water on grease fires, not to rely on closing the lid, and to turn knobs and LP tank off.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:384-400` supports hose and valve checks before use.
- `Outdoor Grill.md:480-503` covers food safety: clean, separate, cook, chill, use a meat thermometer, and never partially grill meat or poultry to finish later.
