Question: When I am sailing, how do I check the engine oil level to ensure continued sailing?

Manual guess: `Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md`

Evidence:

- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:873-875` says a Low Oil Pressure warning appears on the multi-function display with the buzzer, engine speed is automatically limited, and the operator should reduce engine speed, return to shore or maneuver to a safe location, check engine oil level, add oil if low, or have a Boat Dealer check the engine if the level is sufficient.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:1569` notes that high-speed engine operation may consume engine oil and says to check the engine oil level.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:1575-1585` warns that engine oil is extremely hot immediately after stopping, says not to run with too much or too little oil, says to keep debris and water out of the oil tank filler hole, and says that on water the boat should be moored so it will not drift away.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:1587-1597` gives the procedure: stop/level or launch the boat, start the engine after looking in all directions, idle for 6 minutes or more plus 5 additional minutes if ambient temperature is 20 C (68 F) or less, stop it, open the hood, remove and wipe the oil tank filler cap/dipstick, screw it in until it stops, remove it again, verify the level between minimum and maximum marks, add oil slowly if low, repeat until proper, secure the cap, repeat for the other engine, and close the hood.
