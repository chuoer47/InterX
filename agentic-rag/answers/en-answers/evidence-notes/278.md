Question: How should fire extinguishers be stored on board and where should they be placed?

Manual guess: Yamaha 210FSH 2021

Evidence:

- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:1727-1729` states that the boat must be fitted with one 5-B (B-1) type fire extinguisher when navigating waters controlled by the U.S. Coast Guard, that at least one extinguisher should be aboard and full, and that two are recommended.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:1731-1737` states that one fire extinguisher is mounted in the lockable storage compartment and should be a chemical-type extinguisher of two pounds or more. If two extinguishers are used, the other is mounted in the battery compartment at the location label outside the compartment; because it is near the engine compartment, it should be a clean-agent type such as CO2 or FE-36 with a capacity of five pounds or more.

Final content:

Keep at least one full 5-B (B-1) fire extinguisher aboard; two are recommended. Mount one extinguisher in the lockable storage compartment, using a chemical-type extinguisher with a capacity of 2 lb or more.<PIC>
If a second extinguisher is carried, mount it in the battery compartment at the labeled location outside the compartment.<PIC> Because that extinguisher is near the engine compartment, use a clean-agent type that displaces oxygen, such as CO2 or FE-36, with a capacity of 5 lb or more.
