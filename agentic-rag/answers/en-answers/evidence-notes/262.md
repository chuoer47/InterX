Question: How to flush the cooling system of the boat?

Manual guess: Yamaha 210FSH 2021

Evidence:
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:2061-2063` says post-operation care should be done after the boat is loaded back on the trailer, proper flushing is not possible with the boat in the water, and the cooling system should be flushed to prevent salt, sand, or dirt clogging.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:2130-2145` gives the first flushing steps: connect the garden hose adapter to a hose, remove the flush hose connector cap, install the adapter by pushing and twisting, connect to a water tap, start the engine, then immediately turn the water fully on until water flows from the jet thrust nozzle and cooling water pilot outlet. It warns never to turn water on before starting the engine.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md:2147-2154` gives the remaining steps: run at fast idle for 3 to 5 minutes, turn water off immediately if the engine stops, turn water off before draining residual exhaust water by moving the remote control lever up to half throttle and back to idle for 10 to 15 seconds, stop the engine, remove the adapter, and securely tighten the cap. It warns not to have water on when the engine is not running and not to run more than 15 seconds after water is turned off.
