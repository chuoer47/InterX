Question: How the ship steers?

Manual guess: Yamaha 210FSH 2021

Evidence:

1. `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md` lines 658-671:
   The Steering section says the boat is steered by turning the steering wheel in the direction of travel. Turning the wheel changes the angle of the rear jet thrust nozzles; the articulating keel changes direction with the nozzles. It also says the throttle must be opened above idle for turning except at trolling speed, and that the boat steers from the stern so the stern swings opposite the turn.

2. `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md` lines 1963-1981:
   The Turning the boat section says steering control depends on steering wheel position plus throttle. Water is pressurized by impellers and expelled through jet thrust nozzles to move and steer the boat. More throttle gives higher thrust and sharper turns; less throttle gives lower thrust and gradual turns. At idle/neutral above trolling speed, steering ability rapidly decreases; at trolling speed, idle thrust can turn the boat gradually. If the engines are stopped or there is no thrust, the boat goes straight even if the wheel is turned.

Answer basis:

The answer combines the direct control explanation from the Steering section with the operational limitation from Turning the boat: the boat is jet-thrust steered, so steering needs thrust except for gradual trolling-speed turns on idle thrust.
