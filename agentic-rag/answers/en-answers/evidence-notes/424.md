# Evidence Notes: 424

Question: What are the correct techniques for crossing a slope on a snowmobile?

Manual: `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha Snowmobile.md`

Evidence:

1. `Yamaha Snowmobile.md:1099` identifies the relevant section as `CROSSING A SLOPE (SIDE HILL)`.
2. `Yamaha Snowmobile.md:1101-1103` warns that side hills are not recommended for beginner or novice snowmobilers and explains that crossing a slope requires weight positioning for balance.
3. `Yamaha Snowmobile.md:1103` says to lean the body so weight is toward the uphill side, with the recommended position of the downhill-leg knee on the seat and the uphill-leg foot on the running board.
4. `Yamaha Snowmobile.md:1103` says that if the snowmobile slips sideways, steer in the direction of the slide if no obstacles are in the path, then gradually steer back toward the intended direction as balance returns.
5. `Yamaha Snowmobile.md:1103` says that if the snowmobile starts to tip, steer down the hill to regain balance.
6. `Yamaha Snowmobile.md:1105` provides the adjacent illustration `Manual34_129`, which shows the rider shifted uphill in the recommended side-hill posture.
7. `Yamaha Snowmobile.md:1107-1113` says to dismount immediately on the uphill side if correct balance cannot be maintained and the snowmobile is going to tip over, and to use slow, cautious operation on ice or icy surfaces while avoiding rapid acceleration, turning, or braking.
