# 291 Evidence Notes

Question: After or during shooting, how can I view the camera image on TV for better clarity?

Manual: Canon EOS 20D

Evidence:
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Canon EOS 20D.md:773-777` says the LCD monitor cannot be used as a viewfinder for shooting.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Canon EOS 20D.md:2821-2829` says captured images can be viewed on a TV with the provided video cable, with both camera and TV turned off before connecting or disconnecting, then connecting the camera terminal to the TV monitor's VIDEO IN terminal and switching the TV line input to Video IN.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Canon EOS 20D.md:2831` anchors `Manual10_182.jpg`, which shows the cable connection to Video IN.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Canon EOS 20D.md:2833-2839` says to set the camera power switch to `<ON>`, press the playback button, and that the image appears on the TV while nothing is displayed on the camera LCD monitor. It anchors `Manual10_183.jpg`, which shows the playback/power controls.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Canon EOS 20D.md:2841` says the proper video system format must be set with `[Video system]`, and the TV may cut off the image periphery.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Canon EOS 20D.md:3902-3905` confirms TV display failures can be caused by video cable plugs not inserted fully or by the wrong NTSC/PAL video format.

Image decision:
- Use `Manual10_182` for the cable and TV VIDEO IN connection.
- Use `Manual10_183` for the power switch and playback button step.

Final answer summary:
Use the supplied video cable to connect the EOS 20D to the TV VIDEO IN input with both devices off, switch the TV input to Video IN, turn the camera on, and press playback. The captured image appears on the TV and the camera LCD stays blank. This is for captured-image review, not live shooting viewfinder use; match the camera video system to the TV if display is improper.
