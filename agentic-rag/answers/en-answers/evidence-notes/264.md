# Question 264

Question: How to check the engine oil level of the boat?

Manual: `agentic-rag/en-manual/Yamaha 210FSH 2021.md`

Evidence:

1. Lines 1575-1585 warn that engine oil is extremely hot immediately after shutdown, that too much or too little oil can damage the engine, and that debris or water must not enter the oil tank filler hole. They also state that land checks require cooling-water supply while the engine runs, and water checks require mooring the boat.
2. Lines 1587-1597 give the procedure: place the boat precisely level on land or launch it, start the engine after looking around, idle for 6 minutes or more, add 5 minutes if ambient temperature is 20 deg C (68 deg F) or less, stop the engine, open the hood, remove and wipe the oil tank filler cap/dipstick, screw it back in until it stops, remove it, verify the oil is between minimum and maximum marks, slowly add oil if low, consult a Boat Dealer if significantly high, reinstall the cap, repeat for the other engine, and close the hood.
3. Lines 1591-1597 anchor `Manual09_196` to the oil tank filler cap/dipstick and `Manual09_197` to the dipstick maximum and minimum marks.

Image use:

1. `Manual09_196`: identifies the oil tank filler cap/dipstick to remove and wipe.
2. `Manual09_197`: identifies the dipstick marks that define the correct oil level range.
