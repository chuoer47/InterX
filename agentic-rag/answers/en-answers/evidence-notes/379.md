Question: How can you create RAID of a motherboard?

Manual guess: `Motherboard.md`

Evidence:
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Motherboard.md:583` states that Serial ATA hard disk drives connected through the onboard Intel chipset can create RAID 0, 1, 5, and 10 configurations.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Motherboard.md:591` says Intel SATA connectors default to AHCI and SATA Mode should be set to RAID when creating a Serial ATA RAID set.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Motherboard.md:943` states that EZ Tuning Wizard can easily set RAID in the system.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Motherboard.md:963` through `agentic-rag/en-manual/Motherboard.md:991` gives the creation procedure: open EZ Tuning Wizard with `<F11>`, click RAID and Yes, confirm disks have no existing RAID volumes and are connected to Intel SATA connectors, click Next when disks appear, choose Easy Backup or Super Speed, choose RAID1/RAID10 or RAID0/RAID5 as applicable, confirm setup, exit, and reset.
- `agentic-rag/en-manual/Motherboard.md:1503` through `agentic-rag/en-manual/Motherboard.md:1521` confirms SATA Mode Selection includes RAID Mode for SATA hard disk RAID and that RAID 0, RAID 1, RAID 10, and RAID 5 can be enabled.
